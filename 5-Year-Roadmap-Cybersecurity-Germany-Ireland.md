# 5-Year Roadmap: Cybersecurity Career → Masters (Germany/Ireland) → Job by 2030-31

**Starting point:** Absolute beginner in cybersecurity | Graduated 2025 | Currently job hunting
**Target:** Land a cybersecurity job in Germany or Ireland by 2030-2031

---

## YEAR 1 (Aug 2026 – Aug 2027): Foundation + India Job

**Goal:** Get ANY job (even non-cybersecurity IT/support role) for income + experience, while building cybersecurity fundamentals on the side.

| Area | Action |
|---|---|
| Job | Land a job in India (IT support, networking, junior SOC, helpdesk — anything in tech). Don't wait for "perfect" cybersecurity role. |
| Cybersecurity | Google Cybersecurity Certificate → Networking concepts (Messer) → TryHackMe (Pre Security + Cyber Security 101 paths) |
| English | Daily speaking practice + grammar/tense basics + technical reading (start reading tech news daily) |
| Savings | Start a separate savings account — target ₹1-1.5 Lakh saved by year end |
| Networking | Build LinkedIn profile properly + start a GitHub/portfolio repo showing your learning |

**End of Year 1 checkpoint:** You should have a job + basic networking/security fundamentals + TryHackMe profile with visible progress.

---

## YEAR 2 (Aug 2027 – Aug 2028): Build Real Skills + Certification

**Goal:** Move from "learning" to "employable junior cybersecurity skillset." Get first real cybersecurity-adjacent job or role switch.

| Area | Action |
|---|---|
| Cybersecurity | CompTIA Security+ certification (industry-recognized entry cert) → start Cloud Security basics (AWS/Azure fundamentals) |
| Job | Try to move internally or switch jobs into a SOC Analyst / IT Security role — even L1 SOC counts |
| Language | **If Germany is priority** → Start German A1 (Goethe Institut or online, 3-4 months) |
| English | IELTS prep begins — target band 6.5-7 |
| Finance | Research: blocked account cost is **~€11,904/year (~₹11 Lakh)** — start a dedicated "Germany fund" |
| Research | Shortlist 8-10 universities (Germany: TU Berlin, RWTH Aachen, Saarland, Bonn-Rhein-Sieg; Ireland: UCD, Trinity, DCU, UL) — check cybersecurity MSc entry requirements |

**End of Year 2 checkpoint:** Security+ certified, 1+ year real security-related job experience, A1 German (if Germany track), IELTS attempt done.

---

## YEAR 3 (Aug 2028 – Aug 2029): Applications + Visa Process

**Goal:** Get admission + visa sorted. This is the "paperwork heavy" year.

| Area | Action |
|---|---|
| Language | German A2-B1 complete (if Germany track) |
| Cybersecurity | Continue working, aim for SOC Analyst / Cloud Security role with 2 years experience by now |
| Applications | Apply to shortlisted universities (Germany deadlines: mostly **Jan for Summer intake, July for Winter intake**) |
| APS Certificate | Mandatory for German visa (Indian students) — apply early, takes 3-5 weeks, costs ~₹18,000 |
| Blocked Account | Open with Expatrio/Fintiba once admission confirmed — deposit ~€11,904 |
| Visa | Apply for German/Irish student visa once admit + blocked account + APS are ready |
| Savings | Should have blocked account amount + buffer (~₹13-14 Lakh total) ready by mid-year |

**End of Year 3 checkpoint:** University admit letter in hand, visa approved, ready to fly by late 2029.

---

## YEAR 4 (Aug 2029 – Aug 2030): Masters + Strategic Internship

**Goal:** Study + use the mandatory internship (common in German/Irish masters) as your job pipeline.

| Area | Action |
|---|---|
| Studies | Focus coursework on cybersecurity specialization (SOC ops, cloud security, DevSecOps) |
| Internship | German/Irish masters usually include a mandatory internship (Praxissemester) — **target companies you'd want to work for full-time** |
| Networking | Attend local meetups, career fairs, LinkedIn networking in-country — this matters more abroad than in India |
| Language | If in Germany, push German to B2 — massively improves job chances even in English-speaking tech roles |
| Job search | Start applying for post-masters roles in your final semester, not after |

**End of Year 4 checkpoint:** Internship converted to job offer, or strong applications in pipeline before graduation.

---

## YEAR 5 (Aug 2030 – 2031): Land the Job

**Goal:** Convert to full-time role, transition to post-study work visa.

| Area | Action |
|---|---|
| Visa | Germany: switch to EU Blue Card / job seeker visa. Ireland: Stamp 1G (2-year post-study work permission) |
| Job | Target roles: SOC Analyst → Cloud Security → mid-level Security Engineer |
| Salary reality check | Entry-level in Germany: ~€45-55k/year (~₹40-50L). This is your realistic Year 5 number — the ₹70L target is more of a **Year 7-8 milestone**, not Year 5. |
| Long-term | Once settled, target Blue Card → PR track in Germany, or Stamp 4 in Ireland |

---

## Key Corrections to Your Original Plan

1. **Ireland doesn't need German** — only pursue German if Germany is the primary target. Don't split focus.
2. **50-70 Lac/year is a Year 7-8 number, not Year 5** — fresh masters graduates start around ₹40-50L in Germany/Ireland; the higher number comes after 2-3 years of experience there.
3. **APS Certificate** (Germany-specific, Indian students) — you had blocked account noted but this was missing. It's mandatory and takes time.
4. **University application deadlines are rigid** — Jan/July cycles. Miss it and you lose a full year. This is why Year 3 has to be tightly planned.
